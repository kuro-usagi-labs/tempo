# UI Design System

## Design direction

Modern dark wellness interface: minimal, tactile, calm, and premium. It should feel closer to a high-end fitness or breathing app than an explicit sexual-health utility.

## Visual personality

- deep near-black background;
- layered charcoal surfaces;
- restrained indigo/cyan progress accents;
- amber for caution;
- saturated red only for immediate stop/safety states;
- soft depth and glass-like material where supported;
- large typography and generous spacing;
- rounded geometric controls;
- no anatomical illustrations on the home screen.

## Core layout rules

- 8-point spacing grid.
- Minimum 44 × 44 pt touch targets.
- Primary actions near the lower thumb zone.
- One dominant action per screen.
- Avoid dense dashboards.
- Keep session controls usable with one hand.

## Navigation

Use a dark floating tab bar with a subtle material effect. During guided sessions, hide the tab bar and enter an immersive focus mode.

## Cards

- 20–28 pt corner radius depending on size.
- Fine inner highlight, minimal external shadow.
- Surface hierarchy comes from luminance, blur, and border opacity.
- Cards lift 2–4 pt on touch and return with a spring.

## Typography

Use SF Pro system typography.

- Display: 34–44 pt, rounded where appropriate.
- Title: 24–28 pt semibold.
- Body: 16–18 pt regular.
- Metric: monospaced digits.
- Caption: 12–14 pt medium.

## Tone

Direct, calm, non-clinical, non-erotic, and never patronizing.
