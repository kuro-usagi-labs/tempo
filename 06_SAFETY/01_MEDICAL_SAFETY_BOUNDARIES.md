# Medical Safety Boundaries

TEMPO is a self-guided wellness and skill-training product. It must not diagnose or guarantee treatment.

## Stop-training red flags

- blood in semen or urine;
- fever;
- severe genital, testicular, pelvic, or abdominal pain;
- urinary burning;
- unusual cloudy, yellow, green, or foul-smelling discharge;
- acute injury, swelling, bruising, or suspected fracture;
- persistent pain during ejaculation;
- sudden major erectile change with other symptoms.

## System response

- Block guided sexual sessions.
- Show clear in-app guidance to seek an appropriate clinician.
- Keep neutral copy in notifications.
- Allow exercise only when unrelated and safe; otherwise recommend rest.

## Acquired change

When a user reports that control was previously normal and has changed substantially, the app should recommend clinical assessment if the problem persists or is distressing, especially with urinary, pelvic, pain, or erectile symptoms.

## Pelvic-floor guidance

Do not automatically prescribe aggressive Kegel routines. Use relaxation and body awareness as default. Pelvic pain or urinary symptoms require professional assessment.

## Medication and topical products

The app may explain that clinicians sometimes use medical treatments, but must not:

- prescribe dosing;
- recommend tramadol;
- recommend unregulated numbing creams;
- suggest toothpaste, balm, oil, or irritants;
- tell users to start or stop prescription medicines.

## Crisis and emergency

The app is not an emergency service. Severe pain, major injury, heavy bleeding, fainting, or other acute danger should display immediate emergency-care guidance appropriate to the localization.
