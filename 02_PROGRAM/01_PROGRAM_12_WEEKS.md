# Automatic 12-Week Program

The program is competency-based. Weeks provide structure, but advancement depends on safe completion and skill metrics.

## Phase 0 — Safety and baseline

**Duration:** onboarding + first 2–3 days

- Confirm adult status.
- Complete symptom screening.
- Establish baseline habits and anxiety.
- Avoid sexual training if a red flag exists.
- Introduce arousal scale and pre-ejaculate education.

## Phase 1 — Awareness

**Weeks 1–2**

Goals:

- recognize arousal levels 1–10;
- identify tension in jaw, abdomen, thighs, buttocks, and pelvic area;
- pause before level 8;
- avoid rushing and repeated daily testing.

Default weekly plan:

- 2 guided awareness sessions;
- 3 short breathing/relaxation tasks;
- 2 cardio tasks;
- 1 beginner strength task;
- 1 weekly review.

Progression gate:

- at least 3 safe sessions;
- user can identify escalation before “almost too late” in 2 sessions;
- no unresolved pain or injury.

## Phase 2 — Basic control

**Weeks 3–4**

Goals:

- complete 2–3 controlled pause cycles;
- reduce stimulation at level 6;
- stop at adaptive threshold 6–7;
- recover toward level 3–4.

Default weekly plan:

- 2 control sessions;
- optional third session only after good recovery and no irritation;
- 2 cardio tasks;
- 2 strength or mobility tasks;
- 2 relaxation tasks.

Progression gate:

- 3 cycles completed in at least 2 sessions;
- late-stop rate below 40%;
- anxiety not trending sharply upward.

## Phase 3 — Stability

**Weeks 5–8**

Goals:

- maintain control across changing tempo;
- shorten recovery without forcing it;
- reduce dependence on visual prompts;
- avoid overly tight grip and body bracing.

Changes:

- haptic-only mode introduced;
- some sessions hide the running duration;
- target cycles can rise to 4–5;
- assistance fades after consistent success.

Progression gate:

- stable results across 3 sessions;
- user reports moderate or good confidence;
- no compulsive increase in training frequency.

## Phase 4 — Transfer

**Weeks 9–10**

For users with partners, optional:

- communicate a pause signal;
- reduce pressure around penetration;
- use graded, non-goal-oriented intimacy;
- practice stopping earlier than usual.

For solo users:

- varied context without pornography;
- reduced app prompts;
- self-directed control checks.

## Phase 5 — Independence

**Weeks 11–12**

Goals:

- use the app mainly for review;
- maintain exercise and sleep routines;
- perform sessions without watching a timer;
- know when to rest and when to seek help.

Completion criteria:

- user shows improved awareness/control trend or reports meaningful confidence improvement;
- no unresolved safety flags;
- user can explain their own pause threshold and recovery strategy.

The app should present completion as “graduation from guided mode,” not medical cure.
