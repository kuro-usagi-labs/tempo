# Exercise and Recovery Program

Exercise supports general cardiovascular health, mood, sleep, and stress regulation. The app must not claim that jogging or push-ups directly cure ejaculation problems.

## Baseline assessment

Ask:

- current weekly movement;
- ability to walk continuously for 20 minutes;
- injuries or medical restrictions;
- access to safe outdoor space;
- preferred activities.

## Cardio progression

### Level 0 — Inactive

- 10–15 minute easy walk, 2 times/week.
- Add 5 minutes when sessions feel manageable.

### Level 1 — Beginner

- 20–25 minute brisk walk, 2–3 times/week.
- Optional intervals: 1 minute easy jog + 2 minutes walk.

### Level 2 — Developing

- 25–35 minutes, 3 times/week.
- Mix brisk walking, jogging, cycling, or skipping.

### Level 3 — Maintenance

Move toward general adult-health guidance: 150–300 minutes moderate activity weekly or equivalent vigorous activity, as appropriate.

## Strength progression

Start 1 day/week, progress toward 2 days/week.

### Beginner circuit

- wall or incline push-up: 2 × 6–10;
- chair squat: 2 × 8–12;
- glute bridge: 2 × 8–12;
- bird dog: 2 × 6 each side;
- calf raise: 2 × 10–15.

### Progression rules

- Add repetitions before adding sets.
- Stop for sharp pain, dizziness, chest pain, or unusual shortness of breath.
- Avoid presenting exercise as punishment for masturbation.

## Relaxation

Use down-training rather than automatic hard Kegel prescriptions:

- diaphragmatic breathing;
- relaxed jaw and shoulders;
- pelvic-area release cue;
- gentle hip mobility;
- body scan.

Users with pelvic pain, urinary symptoms, or persistent tension should be directed to a clinician rather than receiving increasingly intense pelvic exercises.
